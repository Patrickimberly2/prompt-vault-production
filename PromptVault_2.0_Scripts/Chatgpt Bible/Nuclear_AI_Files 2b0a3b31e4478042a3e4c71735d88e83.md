**1. How to make ChatGPT more creative**

Something most ChatGPT users ignore is actually what makes it super powerful. **ChatGPT parameters.**

More specifically, to give your ChatGPT superpowers you need to know about three fundamental parameters: temperature, diversity_penalty, and best_of.

There would be a long list of parameters:

But for now it‚Äôs important for you to focus on these three:

**a) temperature**

Typically ranging from 0 to 1. It affects the randomness of the model‚Äôs output. A higher value results in more diverse outputs. A lower value makes the output more deterministic and focused.

‚óè Low temperature (0-0.3): focused, consistent, conservative outputs.

‚óè Medium temperature (0.3-0.7): balanced creativity and coherence/consistency.

‚óè High temperature (0.7-1): highly creative and diverse, maybe less coherent.

![](image2%206.png)

For example, when editing books, it is often preferable to set a low temperature in order to maintain consistency rather than excessive creativity in the writing style and content.

**b) diversity_penalty**

Typically ranging from 0 to 2. It encourages the model to produce a diverse set of responses by penalising repeated tokens.

‚óè low diversity penalty (0): no penalty applied, might have repetitive phrases.

‚óè medium diversity penalty (1): balance between diversity and coherence.

‚óè high diversity penalty (2): maximum diversity, might affect coherence. It will try to invent new ways of thinking.

For example, when using ChatGPT to reformat large text for articles and books, it is often necessary to reduce the occurrence of repetitive phrases.

**c) best_of**

The range can include any positive number. It instructs the model to generate a specified number of responses and return the best one according to its internal scoring. By iterating and connecting data, the aim is to produce acceptable or optimal cases of data that align with user expectations.
