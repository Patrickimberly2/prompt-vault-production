# Guidelines

Making a video that really grabs attention is all about the little details—how the camera moves, how the lighting sets the mood, and how the overall style makes it stand out. 

These guidelines are here to help you create something amazing, whether you’re going for a dramatic feel, a fun vibe, or something super creative.

1. **Include details about camera movement** 
- Camera slowly pans right/left
- Camera tilts upward to reveal the sky
- Tracking shot following a moving character
- Steady overhead drone shot
- Low-angle shot looking upward at the subject
- High-angle shot looking downward
- 360-degree orbit around the subject
- POV shot mimicking a character’s perspective
- Handheld camera for a natural, shaky effect
- Slow push-in to emphasize emotion
- Camera zooms out to reveal a vast landscape
- Over-the-shoulder perspective

1. **Specify lighting** 
- Golden hour lighting for warm
- Blue hour lighting for cool
- Rim lighting (creating a silhouette effect)
- Neon lighting
- Lens flare effect (cinematic look)
- Subtle ambient lighting
- Dramatic, moody lighting
- Studio lighting

1. **Indicate the pace of action** 
- Slow motion
- Timelapse
- Hyperlapse
- Normal speed
- Freeze-frame

1. **Add visual styles** 
- Photorealistic
- Minimalist
- Abstract
- Animation style
- Cinematic
- Hyperrealistic
- Retro 80s aesthetic
- Futuristic cyberpunk
- Dreamlike visuals
- Black and white
