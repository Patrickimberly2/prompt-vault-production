# Brainstorming Questions for Job Candidates

### FILL-IN-THE-BLANK **PROMPTS:**

```jsx
I am about to interview the candidates for the [**role you want to fill**] in my [**coaching/consulting**] business. For this position I am looking for someone [**brief description**]. The tasks they will have to do are: [**brief list of the tasks**].
What questions can I ask candidates to see if they are a good fit for the role?
```

```jsx
I am looking for a new [**role you want to fill**], but I don't want to work with people who [**traits you want to avoid**]. What questions should I ask my candidates to make sure I avoid that kind of people?
```

```jsx
What are the best questions I can ask [**category of the position you want to fill**] to see if we're a good fit?
```

### QUESTIONS-BASED P**ROMPTS:**

1. "What are some effective strategies for brainstorming questions to ask job candidates for a coaching business?"
2. "How can I ensure that the questions I am asking are relevant to the position and reflect the needs of my coaching business?"
3. "What are some key qualities I should be looking for in a job candidate, and how can I develop questions that will help me assess those qualities?"
4. "How can I balance asking open-ended questions that allow for creative answers with more structured questions that help me evaluate specific skills and experience?"
5. "What are some best practices for structuring interview questions in a way that is clear and easy to understand for job candidates?"
6. "How can I use behavioral-based interview questions to assess a candidate's past experiences and how they may be applied to the position in my coaching business?"
7. "What are some ways to make sure that my interview questions are fair and unbiased to all candidates?"
8. "How can I incorporate questions that assess a candidate's fit with my coaching business's culture and values?"
9. "What are some common mistakes to avoid when brainstorming interview questions for a coaching business?"
10. "How can I use feedback from previous interviews and employee performance to inform the questions I ask in future interviews?"
